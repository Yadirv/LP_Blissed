Glossary of Technical Terms

Microcomedone
Invisible microscopic lesion representing the initial stage of every acne lesion. It's not just a physical "plug" but an active immunological organ where the inflammatory cascade begins up to 8 weeks before a visible pimple appears.
Interleukin-1 alpha (IL-1Î±)
Pro-inflammatory cytokine that spikes by 320% in pores that still look normal, signaling the start of the immune response in the invisible phase of acne. It's one of the first detectable markers of the inflammatory process.
Filotype IA1
Virulent genetic subtype of the Cutibacterium acnes bacteria specifically associated with inflammatory acne. Unlike other beneficial strains, this filotype activates pro-inflammatory immune pathways in skin cells.
CLR Pathway (C-type Lectin Receptor Pathway)
Innate immune signaling system that C. acnes IA1 bacteria activate in keratinocytes, triggering the release of inflammatory signals hours after bacterial contact and perpetuating the inflammatory response.
Hub Genes
Central controller genes in gene expression networks such as TNFA, IL8, IL1A, and MMP9, that regulate multiple inflammatory pathways and act as critical nodes in the genetic response to acne.
Linoleic Acid
Essential omega-6 fatty acid whose deficiency in sebum is characteristic of acne-prone skin. Its absence causes sebum to become thick and sticky instead of fluid, contributing to pore obstruction and weakening of the skin barrier.
Lipid Dysregulation
Alteration in sebum lipid composition, not just in quantity but in quality. Includes reduction of linoleic acid and increase of comedogenic lipids, creating an environment conducive to inflammation and bacterial proliferation.
Transcriptomics
Branch of genomics that studies the complete set of messenger RNA (mRNA) transcripts in a cell, allowing identification of which genes are actively being expressed in response to specific stimuli such as bacterial infection in acne.
